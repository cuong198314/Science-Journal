Content experiment

Excited to share the data results up to this point.

Posted by Cuong Mai on May June 7, 2020

The site that I am setting my goal for tracking is CM Foodies

My goal is to track users engagement on my site/subpages, I would like to see if any users will engage at least 5 minutes or more on my site/subpages.

To achieve my goal, users would need to browse and engage on my site/subpages for at least 5 minutes or more. On my site/subpages there are multiple videos for users to watch as well as pictures and comments to view.

Here are the results and data from Google analytics and MonsterInsights.

What I've learned up to this point from the data are the goal I set up to get users stay on my site for at least five minutes or more failed and that I need more and better contents on my site to keep the users stay longer. The average user engage on the site for only about one minute, that is four minutes short of my goal of five minutes.

At this point I did not or haven't made any changes to the content of my experiment to improve users engagement.

The process of setting up the content experiment (goal experiment) is not too bad, at first I don't know if I've set up correctly to collect the data of users engagement of the site. I am not sure if my setting is correct for Google anylytics to record and track users that stay at least 5 minutes or more on my site.

In order to test if my goal experiment is set up correctly, I ask my niece to try the site and stay for at least 5 minutes and longer to see if my users engagement experiment will record and track correctly. After a few days have passed I check the data but don't see any data of users engagement of 5 minutes or more recorded.

The two pages with the most views are the Italian and Asian page.

To improve on users engagement, I will need more and better contents to the site such as more pictures, more details in the reviews and possibly actual customers review in additionl to my own reviews.
