Blogging About Usability Testing via mobile device

Excited to share my thoughts and findings

Posted by Cuong Mai on May 03, 2020

The site/domain that I am covering for this report is Amazon mobile shopping website.

The purpose of this report is to get an idea of how well does the Amazon site is for shopping experience via a mobile device. I observed two people using their mobile devices (androids) to access the site and the findings are below.

A little background from both testers, tester 1 is a hardwoord floor business owner, spend about 25-45 hours a week online, and likes Amazon, Costco, Home depot sites. Tester 2 is an administrative assistant, spend about 20-40 hours a week online, and like to visit news websites such as Yahoo and MSN

Using Krug's testing script as my guideline to record these findings.

Here are the findings

The homepage tour, both testers know about the site as they are regular shoppers, the site homepage laybout is easy to understand for both testers.

The tasks, I asked both testers to not use the search function to navigate to the item they buy often and to an item they would rarely buy.

Tester 1 have no problem navigating to the baby section to buy baby foods and no problem navigating to the men clothing using the drop down menu locating on the top left of the site.

Tester 2 also no problem navigating to the household items and to the face masks section.

The probe, I asked four questions to each tester.

Is the site easy to use and navigate?

Tester 1- Yes, the site is easy like the apps except have to sign in.

Tester 2- Yes

Does the site provide enough info on an item you are buying?

Tester 1- Yes.

Tester 2- Yes and no, oversea items sometimes do not have enough info.

Do you encountered any problems while shopping?

Tester 1- Yes, the site required input of location to check availability of an item, the apps does not.

Tester 2- Yes, have to check if the item is from Amazon to get two days shipping.

What improvements do the site need?

Tester 1- Free membership and less than two hours delivery.

Tester 2- Remove oversea seller, take to long to deliver.

Conclusion

The mobile site homepage layout is very clear and well define for users to follow and navigate from page to page to quickly find the item they want to buy. The drop down menu and the search function allow easy access to the product. The speed of the site and each page is very fast, no lagging or errors observed. Customers would like the site to remember their location without having to enter each time using the site. The color and the text of the site are easy to see and read. The site and placement of the pictures are also nicely place and sizes that are not too distracting to the eyes. There should be a standard of information required for each item to list so that shoppers don't have to think and try to find the info else where. Overall the site and page layout doesn't ovewhelm the users while shopping and the users/testers are satisfied with the site when shopping from their mobile device.
