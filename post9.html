Summary of Google analytics to track my site

Excited to share my thoughts and findings

Posted by Cuong Mai on May 24, 2020

The site that I am tracking is CM Foodies

Some of the questions I would ask myself about my site with regard to tracking of the site are, age group would visit most often, how long will the users stay on the site, what devices would users use to visit the site, and which page is the most popular for users?

To get started, I use Google analytics to track my website and also added the plugin (Google anylytics by MonsterInsights) from my WordPress account and sync it to Google analytics account from Google. To start the tracking, I visit the Google anylytics site Google analytics to register my site and follow instructions from Google anylytics instructions. Once I have everything set up, I ask friends and family members to go to my site so that I can test the tracking. The only realtime data I can access to are active user, type of devices, location, pageview, and traffic sources to the site, other data such as demographic, time spent on the site, and what page is the most browse are not available until later.

My pre-finding of the site

The age group that am expecting to visit my site is all age group, the site is review and recommendation of restaurants in the local area.

Since the site only have information for quick browsing, I don't expect users to stay longer than 15 minutes.

I would think that most users will use mobile device to visit the site.

I think that the Italian food page will be the most visit/popular page.

I also think that my site will also be indirect source of traffic for other sites since I am putting links for users to click and access to those sites.

Conclusion, based on realtime data only, (other data not yet available).

The age group visiting my page is 20-45.

The longest a user spent on my site is about 5 minutes.

All the users visit the site via a mobile device.

The Italian page seem to be the mos popular with users.
